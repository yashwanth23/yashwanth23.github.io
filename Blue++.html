ABOUT

Blue++ is a mobile application that helps users to reduce their plastic footprint in the environment. This app encourages users to use sustainable alternatives to daily use plastics and in return rewards them with discounts and coupons at their favorite cafes and restaurants.

OBJECTIVE

It is overwhelming to see a lot of plastic surrounding us; especially the daily use plastics are ubiquitous and are causing a great damage to our environment. The root cause of the problem lies within the individual's attitude towards the growing plastic consumption. Through this project I wanted to explore the question of “How might we help people make conscious decisions about usage of single-use plastics and help reduce the overall plastic footprint in the environment?”.

I wanted to approach this at an individual level, starting small with just coffee cups and takeout containers. The idea here is to educate and encourage users through a competitive environment to reduce the consumption of plastic by substituting them with alternatives. In return, they would receive rewards and also feel a sense of achievement for creating an impact on the environment because of their choices.

RESEARCH

Plastics are widely available and have increasingly become cheap substitutes for everything. We produce around 335 million tonnes of plastic each year out of which 25 million tonnes of plastic are single use and are dumped in oceans. This is equivalent to one garbage truck dumped every minute in the ocean and it is said to increase to 2 trucks by 2030. This is a massive issue and there are two sides to tackling this. One would be institutionalizing this at a larger scale by banning the use and the other would be bringing change at an individual level in reducing the usage. Though there are a lot of organizations fighting for the cause, this is just removing a glass of water from an ocean. I wanted to approach this issue at an individual level and come up with an unconventional way of solving this.

USER PERSONA
STRATEGY

I incorporated the 3A’s strategy of learning scenario which is Autonomy, Awareness and Action to address this problem.

Autonomy

At first I wanted to understand how people perceive their plastic usage. So, I designed a business card which contains a tally table of commonly used plastics for the entire week. My target audience were mostly students and young professionals who tend to eat outside and drink coffee outside a lot. I handed out these cards to around 10-12 people and collected them after a week. The experiment yielded positive results as the test subjects were able to notice their usage patterns and realize the problem.

Awareness

In terms of awareness, I did an educational piece as I felt that people needed realization of their own plastic usage in general. So I created an audio-visual piece to bring awareness about single-use plastics and how they are impacting our day-to-day lives.



Action

Now that the autonomy and awareness parts are implemented, the cause makes sense only when there is action. In order to do that I was considering several strategies. I came across a podcast that says how peer pressure was used to make people do tasks that they are otherwise uncomfortable to do. The below audio clip is a compilation of various podcasts on how peer pressure was used to tackle the tax delinquencies in the country.

Also, there were some interesting reads on how even small rewards motivate people to do tasks. According to the article “Even tiny rewards can motivate people to go the extra mile” written by Gerhard FurtMuller, Christian Garaus and Wolfgang H. Guttel, individuals seek justification for their efforts and small rewards tend to be more justifiable (concluded based on the experiment they conducted on a group of students).

PRODUCTION

Compiling all the research and the results from the experiment, I decided to make a digital platform to help users take action towards the problem. In return for their effort, discounts and coupons are awarded along with a social status of contributing to the cause. The theme of the mobile app is ocean inspired as this project is about reducing plastic that is being dumped in oceans and in turn helping the sea creatures survive.The name comes from the idea that we wanted to make the ocean as blue as possible.

The user simply needs to login to Blue++ and upload pictures of some common non-plastic reusable items they possess. Once the pictures are approved, they receive a personalized sticker with a QR code. The contains a discount coupon which can be stuck on their item and can be scanned at eligible stores to receive discounts on their purchases.
