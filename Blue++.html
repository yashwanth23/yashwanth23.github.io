Blue++

Product Design | UI/UX Design

ROLE

Researcher, Art director, Interaction designer, Videographer

TEAM

Sole contributor

SKILLS

Information architecture, Prototypes, App screen designs, Interaction design, UX research, Videography and editing

TOOLS

Adobe Photoshop, XD, Premiere Pro

KEYWORDS

Daily use plastics, Sustainable usage, Rewards, Peer pressure

DESCRIPTION

It is overwhelming to see a lot of plastic surrounding us; especially the daily use plastics are ubiquitous. They are increasingly becoming cheap substitutes for everything. Even though there are many sustainable alternatives to plastics available in the market; people prefer using plastics as they are cheap and easily available. Through this project I created an intervention which lets user take a step towards using sustainable alternatives.

HOW IT WORKS?



RESEARCH

I found some interesting podcasts on how peer pressure was used to make people do tasks that they are otherwise uncomfortable to do so. The below audio clip is a complication of various podcasts on how peer pressure was used to tackle the tax delinquencies in the country.

Also, there were some interesting reads on how even small rewards motivate people to do tasks. According to the article “Even tiny rewards can motivate people to go the extra mile” written by Gerhard FurtMuller, Christian Garaus and Wolfgang H. Guttel, individuals seek justification for their efforts and small rewards tend to be more justifiable (concluded based on the experiment they conducted on a group of students).


EXPERIMENT

The target audience for this project are mostly students and young professionals who tend to eat outside and drink coffee outside a lot. Owing to the above research, at first I wanted to understand how people perceive their plastic usage. I wanted to see if people know their contribution to the plastic footprint. I considered a small community of Design and Technology students as my target audience to test my experiment. I designed a business card (figure 1) which contains a tally table of commonly used plastics for the entire week and distributed them to my peers. The experiment in a way worked as the test subjects were noticing their usage patterns and tried to compare with their friends.

I also made a small video to educate people of various forms of daily use plastics that exist and how an average human being uses on a day to day basis. Sounds associated with these daily use plastics are also incorporated to engage the audience with more than one sense (in this case visual and auditory senses).



FINAL PRODUCT

Through the experiment I discovered that people were engaged in participating to record their plastic usage. Hence, I decided to do a digital version and added social aspect to it to encourage reduction of plastic usage through peer pressure. This is done by incorporating personal profile and leaderboard. Since score is given based on the lower usage of plastic, there is always a chance that users might input lower values. To tackle this situation I established a lottery system, the digits of the lottery entry of each user would be the number of different plastic items used in a day. This app was named "Beat the Plastic" which essentially points out to fighting the plastic usage through realization of their usage on a daily basis.

Here is iteration 1


After doing initial tests with users, the feedback I received was that the process was tedious and there was no reward associated with doing such a compelling task. Since this was a self reporting app, users did not find enough motivation to do it on a regular basis. Another thing observed was reporting numbers wouldn't impact the users on a large scale but the problem in hand was a huge one. To cater to these aspects I made few modifications on the previous idea and incorporated reward system to encourage users to participate in using non-plastic alternatives.A list of non-plastic alternatives are provided on the app where users could also make purchases and recieve rewards based on the type of purchase and at the same time recieve points so that they could compete with their friends.

Here is iteration 2


The problem with above iteration was though it was encouraging plastic reduction, it was leading to consumerism, which is another major problem in this country. After several iterations and feedback from users, I finally came up with a version that provides users with discounts and rewards throughout their life if they possess non-plastic alternatives. Also, leaderboard and peer pressure aspects are brought in to broaden the reach.

The user simply needs to login to Blue++ and upload pictures of some common non-plastic reusable items they possess. Once the pictures are approved, they receive a sticker with a QR code that contains discount code which can be stuck on their item and can be scanned at eligible stores to receive discounts on their purchases.
