ABOUT

The Stories Within is a spatial audio experience for storytelling in local journalism. This project is done in collaboration with the R&D team at The New York Times and NYC Media Lab as part of Spatial Computing challenge.

OBJECTIVE

  • How might we create story formats with spatial computing that allow readers to bring journalism into their own space?
  • How might Times journalism appear as a contextual layer on top of the world, via phone-based AR or AR glasses?
  • How might new tools or processes help The Times create spatial content within a fast-paced news cycle? What types of reusable patterns need to exist to support this type of production?

FINAL PRODUCT

This project is developed in three formats: A spatial podcast, A Youtube 360 interactive experience and Bose AR audio experience. Along with the creation of these media, a digital tool has been developed for journalists and reporters to create their own spatial audio stories in all three formats through a simple workflow. For complete information of the project, read the medium publication: The Stories Within.

PROCESS

RESEARCH

The use of AR and VR in the news space creates a new set of interaction methods that have the potential to immerse the reader and engage audiences deeper into the story. Instead of just reading or observing, consumers will increasingly be able to experience news firsthand and establish deeper connections with the stories. Hence we found an opportunity to bring forth this medium and convey stories. We focused on local journalism as we found out it's presence in today's news is diminishing. Especially in times like COVID-19, it is important to maintain community relationships in order to be of help to each other.

Most of the news experiences we see today are are centered around visuals and written content, we realized that audio was being underutilized. In fact, when we think of immersion, the first thing that comes to mind is usually an experience where the visuals dominate and sound is left as an afterthought. Sound is crucial for immersion, it surrounds us in three dimensions in the physical world. With this in mind, we decided to experiment with different ways we can use audio to tell a story. Specifically, we explored spatial audio and leveraged the emotional appeal that comes from listening to a story. So, we took a stand of combining the best of both worlds — the interactivity and spatial awareness of the visual with the convenience and emotional connection of the audio.



APPROACH

In order to realize this project, a journalistic process was adapted wherein it involves finding sources, collecting assets, organizing and producing content in an appropriate medium. For the purpose of case study, Meatpacking district was chosen as it has undergone a drastic shift from meatpacking industry to fashion industry and a lot of local stories were associated to this change.

PRODUCTION

The initial approach was to develop a Bose AR spatial audio experience for people to experience local stories at the location. But home quarantine and restricted outside access due to COVID-19 has opened up new challenges and has led to thinking about accessibility of the location. In order to make this project more reachable given the circumstances, a webVR experience and spatial podcast experience have been developed wherein users can listen to stories sitting at home while still feeling immersed at the location. In total, three experiences are developed that provides various degrees of immersion and accessibility. These three experiences are imagined to be integrated in the New York Times app where users can experience different stories of the series "The Stories Within" in the format they are comfortable to do so.
