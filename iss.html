Interaction sets for strangers

This project was developed by me and my teammate Zihan Shen. The goal of this project is to develop a form that can make strangers interact with it by giving least or no prompt. We have chosen Flatiron plaza (opposite to Flatiron building) as our destination to deploy the project for interaction as it is one of the prime spots in New York city that attracts lot of tourists, artists and photographers. The intention here is to develop relationship with the architectural landmark to oneself by leaving a mark. This project was done in three phases.

Iteration 1: Free Canvas

This iteration was mostly about testing how strangers would interact when they see a free canvas near a landmark. Interestingly we found out that most people were interested in drawing and specifically drawing something related to the landmark. Secondly we also observed that initial interactions with the canvas influenced the later interactions.

Iteration 2: Words stickerboard

The second iteration involves giving certain constraints in the form of words to the participants so that they can share their experiences with Flatiron in the form of sentences. The takeaways from this iteration is that the interaction process was too complex and limited words were provided.

Final iteration: Flatiron comic

Incorporating the learnings from previous iterations we made a 3D model of Flatiron building that contains windows as empty canvases overall giving a look of comic book. It is surprising to see that people interacted with each other through these empty canvases by drawing follow up scenes of previous interactions by other strangers.Also it is interesting to see that participants were signing on these canvases to mark their presence at the location.
